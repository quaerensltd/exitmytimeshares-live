Resale reality

A failed resale attempt can be useful evidence for an exit request

A timeshare may have been sold as flexible, valuable or easy to transfer, but many owners later find that the resale market does not match those promises.

The problem is simple: a buyer usually has to take on the same annual fees, restrictions and booking issues. That makes many timeshares difficult to sell even at a very low price.

If you have tried to sell or transfer the timeshare and had no realistic route forward, that history can help explain why you are now asking the resort for a formal exit or release.

Why resale often fails

Annual fees continue
