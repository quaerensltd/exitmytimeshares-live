Safer first steps

Owners should be careful before paying large upfront fees

The timeshare exit market has attracted companies that make bold promises, charge heavily in advance and leave owners unsure what work has actually been done.

A sensible first step is to organise your evidence, write to the resort clearly and keep proof of every response. That gives you more control and avoids rushing into a costly decision.

ExitMyTimeshares provides a fixed-price DIY document kit, with support available if the timeshare company does not accept the documentation.

Warning signs to watch for

Large upfront fees

Be cautious if the cost is high and the provider cannot explain exactly what will be done.

Guaranteed outcomes

Promises that sound too certain should be checked carefully.

No written process

If the company cannot show a clear document route, evidence plan or follow-up method, pause before paying.
